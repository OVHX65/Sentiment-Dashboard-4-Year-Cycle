Pattern 01
The Post-Peak Denial Window
In every cycle, CT remains stubbornly bullish for 1–2 months after the peak. Dec 2017, Nov 2021, late 2025 — all share the same signature: the crowd consistently fails to recognize the top in real time.
Pattern 02
The Graveyard Phase
Every cycle has a 6–12 month CT dead zone — 2015, 2019, 2023 — where engagement collapses. Low volume, minimal YouTube views. Then quiet accumulation rebuilds, usually triggered by a macro catalyst (halving, ETF rumor, macro pivot).
Pattern 03
FOMO Spike → Fear Inversion
FOMO scores hit 9–10 in the 1–3 months before/at peak, then collapse to 1–2 within 6 months. Fear scores invert: near zero at peak, climbing to 8–10 during capitulation. The FOMO-to-Fear gap averages ~8–10 months across all cycles.
Pattern 04
Narrative Anchoring
Each cycle peak has a dominant CT narrative that justifies the top: ICOs (2017), NFTs/DeFi/institutions (2021), ETFs/Trump/digital gold (2025). CT participants rationalize extreme valuations — then blame the narrative post-crash. Narrative = cope, not signal.
Pattern 05
The Accumulator's Isolation
Sentiment in graveyard years (-2 to 0) is inversely correlated with the best entry prices. 2015 CT was deeply cynical at $152 BTC. 2019 at $3.5k. 2023 post-FTX. The psychological cost of buying in the dead zone explains why almost nobody does it.
Pattern 06
Halving Hype Lag
CT builds halving narratives 6–12 months early, peaks at the event, then immediately cycles through 'priced in' disillusionment for 2–3 months. The actual price impact materializes 6–12 months later — by which point CT has moved to a different narrative entirely.
